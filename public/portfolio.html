Adam Belliveau

Full-Stack Web Developer

Bio:

I'm Adam Belliveau, a 32 year old Web Developer living in Boulder, CO. I recently became a graduate of CodeCraft School of Technology where I learned to take my passion for fixing things into the worlds of jQuery, Node.js, and Angular 2.

Growing up as the child of a software engineer, I first tried to learn C++ at the age of 12. As you might imagine this did not go so well. My childish impatience did not mix well with the idea of hunting down misplaced semi-colons, and I was scared away from coding for two decades.

After spending 5 years working in Human Services as a counselor for traumatized teens, I decided to make a career change. Some soul searching ensued, including a move across the country from Massachusetts to Colorado in pursuit of bigger mountains to climb. Finally I realized that my passion for problem solving, and inate ability to quickly master new skills, was a natural and perfect fit for the world of computer programming.

Outside of personal programming projects my hobbies include mountain biking, rock climbing, and adventure photography.

You can check out my Github Account, view my LinkedIn Profile, or get in touch by email at abellive@me.com

Favorite Projects:

The Most Dangerous GameFull Stack Developer

Description:

This app was created as my final project for CodeCraft. It is a multi-player, geoposition based game where you hunt down your friends in the real world using the GPS in your phone. Last person standing wins.

Technology Used:

The Most Dangerous Game uses a server based on Node.js, Express.js, and Socket.io to handle real-time communication between players as they hunt eachother down. A Mongo database is utilized for persistant storage, and the power of Angular 2 takes care of the front-end framework. Also Compass.js was included to simplify access to the compass built in to most mobile devices for a more engaging user experience.

Where is it now:

Currently in alpha testing at www.themdg.win Designed to be a mobile-only app, once a few more features are fleshed out, and the user experience is streamlined it will be compiled for both iOS and Android.

cubeMazeDeveloper

Description:

This is essentially a few hundred lines of JavaScript to construct a randomized maze of rooms contained within a cube of specified dimensions. It will return an array with a linked list of those rooms, along with an array off the coordinates of all valid rooms within the original cube.

Technology Used:

Vanilla JavaScript

Where is it now:

Up on GitHub free for anyone to use and adapt to their specific needs. I have personally played around with it for the creation of self-generating text adventures, and look forward to utilizing it alongside a 3D game engine in the future.

Mountain Biking

A sport I gave up for years after a pair of gnarly crashes shook my nerves, upon moving to Colorado the bug bit me again. A great passtime on days when climbing partners are hard to find, the beautiful landscapes of Colorado have really inspired me to get out and ride. A big shout out to the Boulder Mountainbike Alliance for all the hard work they put in to make the area a great place for everyone to enjoy.

Heil Valley Ranch is one of my favorite biking locales near Boulder, the Wapiti trail there is a quad burner on the way up, and a blast on the way down. The Ponderosa Loop is fantastically flowy fun with a great view a couple of miles in. The Picture Rock trail can be a great way to get to the top if you want to aboid the burnination caused by the Wapiti Trail and affords a less technical but still wickedly fun descent.
